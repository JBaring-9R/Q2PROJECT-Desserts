Coconut Macaroons

Filipino coconut macaroons are sweet, chewy, coconut-based snacks baked to golden perfection.

How to cook:

1. Preheat oven 350 degrees. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

2. In a large bowl, add egg whites, sugar, salt, almond extract, and vanilla extract.

3. With a hand mixer, beat egg white mixture until thick and frothy, about 2-3 minutes.

4. Add coconut to egg white mixture, and stir until combined.

5. With a small cookie scoop or two spoons, scoop the macaroons into 1" balls. Place on the baking sheet, about 1-2" apart.

6. Bake for about 20-25 minutes, or until the macaroons are golden brown and serve.

Halo-Halo

Halo-halo is a Filipino shaved ice dessert with sweet fruits, beans, and toppings.

How to cook:
1. Blend lime zest, juice, mango nectar, half of mangoes, and a pinch of salt until smooth.
2. Dissolve 1 packet gelatin in ½ cup water, mix with 1 cup mango purée, pour into a loaf pan with mangoes, and chill for 20–30 minutes.
3. Preheat oven to 350°F. Cook sugar with 1 tbsp water until amber, then pour into a loaf pan.
4. Whisk egg yolks, coconut milk, condensed milk, vanilla, and salt; strain into caramel-lined loaf pan, cover with foil, and place in a water bath.
5. Bake flan for 55–60 minutes until set and slightly golden. Let cool, then chill for at least 4 hours.
6. Toss corn with sugar, salt, and oil. Roast for 12–15 minutes and cool.
7. Simmer sago pearls in ½ cup water for 8–10 minutes, drain, mix with coconut milk, and let cool.
8. Toast coconut in a skillet for about 5 minutes until edges brown.
9. Cut and layer flan, mango jelly, ice cream, and toppings like fruits, macapuno, corn, sago, and pinipig.

Leche Flan

Leche flan is a creamy Filipino caramel custard dessert made with egg yolks, milk, and sugar.

How to cook:

1. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).

2. Boil sugar and water over medium-high heat, swirling occasionally, until deep brown (7–10 minutes). Pour into a 10-inch flan mold and tilt to coat the bottom.

3. Combine evaporated milk, condensed milk, egg yolks, and vanilla extract in a large bowl. Stir lightly to prevent bubbles or foam from forming; strain batter.

4. Place the flan mold in a roasting pan lined with foil strips, pour in the custard, and add hot water to the pan halfway up the mold. Cover with foil.

5. Bake for 1 hour until firm, then cool for 1 hour. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours or overnight.

6. Run a knife around the flan, flip onto a platter, and scrape excess caramel over it.

Ube Halaya

Ube halaya is a sweet Filipino dessert made from mashed purple yam, sugar, and coconut milk.

How to cook:
1. Pour coconut milk in a cooking pot. Let boil.
2. Stir and add the condensed milk.
3. Once the mixture starts to boil, add the grated purple yam and ube flavoring. Continue to cook in medium heat while continuously stirring for 30 minutes.
4. Add butter. Stir. Cook until the mixture reaches your desired thickness. (I usually cook it for 10 to 12 minutes after adding butter).
5. Transfer to a container. Top with shredded cheese.
6. Serve. Share and enjoy!

Maja Blanca

Maja blanca is a Filipino coconut milk pudding made with corn, sugar, and cornstarch.

How to cook:

1. Grease a (21x21cm) square pan with butter or margarine. Set aside.

2. Combine coconut milk, milk and condensed milk in a pot and bring to boil over medium heat while stirring occasionally.

3. Add corn, pour in the cornstarch mixture, and stir until thickened. Transfer to a greased pan, tap to remove bubbles, and let cool.

4. Toast the coconut flakes in a pan over low fire until browned. Stir constantly to avoid burning them. Set aside.

5. Sprinkle top with toasted coconut flakes.

Mais Con Yelo

CMais con yelo is a Filipino dessert made with sweet corn, crushed ice, milk, and sugar.

How to cook:

1. Layer the sweet corn and the shaved ice in a glass.

2. Add milk and sugar, condensed milk, evaporated milk (adjust according to taste) and stir with a spoon.

3. Add other ingredients like cheese, saging na saba or cornflakes.

4. Serve immediately.
